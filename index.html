Reading the chart: Countries above the orange dashed line (90%) are meeting the WHO detection target — mostly European or high-income nations. Countries in the bottom-right have high incidence but poor detection: disease is spreading but cases go undiagnosed. Bubble size encodes total case count — a large bubble sitting low on the chart (common in Africa and SE Asia) signals the largest unmet need.
